FORM FOLLOWS NARRATIVE

THESIS: Master of Digital Media, Ryerson University
DATE: Jan - Aug 2015
SUPERVISOR: Dr. Ali Mazalek, CRC Digital Innovation (Synaesthetic Media Lab)
COLLABORATORS: M.McBRIDE, D.HARLEY, J.CHU, O.ZHANG

Design-Research on Experiential Narratives

The impact of the digital age on the design of architectural spaces seldom speaks of narrative experience. Stories shape our entire lives, and dictate the form architecture takes around us. In fact, architecture can be seen as a physical embodiment of human culture. We’ve largely explored how “digital” enables us to build more freely, more complex and more efficiently, but the experiences in virtual space rarely intersect with that of the physical world. How will architecture truly reflect a social life within the digital age?

This major research looked at the application of physical/digital co-design into an architectural mindset, placing research on a digital urbanism into context with the design of a prototype museum exhibit –– where digital media and exhibit design are starting to see cross-overs in the same design process. The prototypes use multisensory embodied design, translating a digital experience and extending it into the design of the physical space, to create opportunities for visitors to connect more meaningfully with culture. Overall, this project posits that ‘Form Follows Narrative’ –– whether digital or physical.

In 2016, the project and co-authored papers were presented, through the Synaesthetic Media Lab at Ryerson and Georgia Tech, at TEI'16 (Eindhoven, NL), MWXX (Los Angeles, US), and DIS'16 (Brisbane, AU).
