WILD FOR BEES

Design-Build Installation, SUSTAINABLE.TO Architecture + Building
DATE: Jan - July 2014
TEAM: P.DOWSETT (Principal), J.KWAN, A.STIFFMAN, J.ANDERSON
LOCATION: FAIRMONT ROYAL YORK HOTEL, TORONTO BOTANICAL GARDENS

Solitary bee habitats made simply and sustainably.

Rapidly facing population decline, the bee hotels strengthen the urban ecology by providing nesting spaces for solitary bees. The structures are built entirely from recycled urban material, while creating a site-specific aesthetic for an educational initiative centered on pollinator health. We wanted to show that bee habitats can be beautiful and easy to build in your own backyard!

This project is an ongoing national project in association with Fairmont Hotel and Resorts, Pollinator Partnership Canada, and Burt's Bees. The structures were built in collaboration with youth from at-risk neighbourhoods, and travelled to local farmer's markets and neighbourhood events as part of the educational campaign.
