org_structure:
  - |
    <p>In grad school, my work sat at the seam between museums/classrooms (physical context), and immersive/digital media (content + interaction). I used a studio-style process: prototype quickly, test with real audiences, then refine the interaction logic until the experience felt intuitive, playful, and emotionally meaningful.</p>
  - |
    <p>Rather than treating "digital" as a layer on top of "space," I treated the space itself as the interface: scent, touch, projected topography, and embodied play became ways to reveal story.</p>

approach: |
  <p>I designed around the idea that differentiated experiences come from combining multiple senses with meaningful digital content. In practice, that meant building interactive reproductions and tabletop systems where discovery happens through <em>touch, presence, and playful exploration</em>, not passive viewing.</p>
  <p>Across projects, I co-designed with stakeholders: kids shaped future entertainment experiences through play-based ideation, while artists and policy makers contributed to "critical play" conversations. The common thread was using workshops and prototypes to turn imagination into interaction, so participants could <em>try</em> the future, not just talk about it.</p>

impact: |
  <p>These efforts demonstrated how physical space can become a storytelling engine for digital and immersive media. They also provided a repeatable playbook for communicating with diverse audiences (learners, museum visitors, and Gen Z) by anchoring engagement in relevant daily life, sensory discovery, and co-created concepts.</p>
