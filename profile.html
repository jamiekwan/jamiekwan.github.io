All stories have a beginning, a middle, and an end.
Here's how my narrative continues to unfold.

In 2013, I started my career in Architecture after graduating from Ryerson University.

Fresh out of design school, I landed a job as a Jr. Architect, focused on immersing myself in the world of sustainable design, passive solar architecture, and community engagement. While most of my days were spent in design development and building permit drawings, I had the opportunity to facilitate youth-engagement workshops with the East Scarborough Storefront—an adaptive re-use community hub designed in part by the neighbourhood residents and kids.

Seeing the impact of participation on the community sparked my interest in the human-side of the built environment.

Simultaneously, I had a growing interest in tech, and designing for digital experiences.

Technology opened up a new I had a passion for integrating digital technologies into architectural spaces, and worked with my friends to design interactive installations—including one that created an infinite starry night at the Bata Shoe Museum.
